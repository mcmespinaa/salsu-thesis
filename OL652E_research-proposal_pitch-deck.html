Imagining Beyond Parameters

AI, Futures Consciousness, and Deeper
Sustainability Interventions in Nordic SMEs

Maria Cecilia Espina
SALSU Programme · Leadership and Organisation for Sustainability

01 — The Problem

Sustainability stays at the surface

Nordic SMEs are indispensable sustainability actors, yet their efforts cluster around energy monitoring, compliance, and cost reduction. AI adoption reinforces this shallow orientation.

99.9%
of Nordic enterprises are SMEs
~40%
of value-added in Sweden
Pattern

AI-based solutions predominantly automate tasks, optimise operations, and reduce costs -- reinforcing parameter-level interventions.

Consequence

Leaders are equipped for optimisation but the institutional environment demands transformation. A leverage gap emerges.

Missing

No study examines whether leaders' imaginative capacities shape how they deploy AI for sustainability -- and at what systemic depth.

Can AI help sustainability leaders intervene beyond parameters -- at the deeper design and intent of our systems?

Three Pillars

Converging theories that ground the investigation

I

Leverage Points

III

Complexity Leadership

03 — The Research Gap

Three Streams, One Missing Link

A

AI Adoption in SMEs

Environmental and organisational factors predict adoption, but outcomes measured only at parameter level.

Badghish & Soomro, 2024; Neirotti et al., 2018
B

Futures Consciousness

FC predicts engagement with future-oriented social behaviours, including pro-environmental and civic actions.

Lalot et al., 2020, 2021
C

Deep Intervention

Leveraging deeper system points requires bridging causal and teleological explanations -- engaging human intent.

Fischer & Riechers, 2019; Riechers et al., 2022
Unexamined

Do leaders with stronger futures consciousness use AI differently? Does this difference manifest as interventions at deeper leverage points?

04 — Central Proposition
Imagination is the missing variable

AI Use

Tool deployment patterns

→

Futures Consciousness

Mediating variable

→

Leverage Depth

Systemic intervention level

If FC enables the cognitive capacity to imagine beyond efficiency, then AI tools that amplify this imagination could help leaders target deeper leverage points. The question shifts from "What can AI do?" to "What does human imagination enable leaders to do with AI?"

05 — Research Design

Dual Perspective

Qualitative depth meets quantitative breadth

Qualitative
RQ1
How do Nordic SME sustainability leaders use AI tools in envisioning and implementing deeper sustainability interventions?
RQ2
What distinguishes imagination-AI interaction at deeper leverage points vs. parameter-level?
Method Multiple case study (4-6 Nordic SMEs)
Sampling Purposive, replication logic (deep vs. shallow)
Data Semi-structured interviews + document analysis
Analysis Thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006) -- deductive + inductive coding
Dual
Quantitative
RQ3
Are FC and AI use leverage depth associated with initiative leverage depth, controlling for firm size, sector, and country?
RQ4
Which FC dimensions show the strongest associations? Does the FC + deep AI combination explain additional variance?
Method Cross-sectional online survey (n = 100-150)
Target Sustainability managers in Nordic SMEs
Instrument Validated 20-item FC Scale (Lalot et al., 2020) + vignettes
Analysis Hierarchical regression, correlation analysis
06 — Research Plan

10-Week Timeline

Weeks 1-2 Literature Review & Instrument Design

Systematic review, interview guide development, survey instrument preparation

Weeks 2-3 Case Recruitment & Pilot Survey

Purposive case selection, cognitive pilot interviews (n = 5-8) for measurement validity

Weeks 4-7 Data Collection

Semi-structured interviews with case study SMEs; survey distribution across Nordic region

Weeks 7-8 Analysis

Thematic analysis with deductive/inductive coding; hierarchical regression analysis

Weeks 8-10 Writing, Revision & Submission

Manuscript drafting, peer review, final revisions, and submission

Ethical Considerations

Swedish Research Council guidelines (Vetenskapsradet, 2017): informed consent, pseudonymised recordings, anonymous surveys, encrypted university data storage.

07 — Practical Contribution

Who Benefits

◆

SME Leaders

Concrete models of how leaders combine imagination with AI. Clarity on which FC dimensions enable deeper intervention.

From "Does this tool improve performance?" to "Is this tool helping me imagine and redesign?"
◇

Policymakers

Conditions under which AI becomes a tool for transformation rather than optimisation. Direct implications for sustainability training, AI adoption guidance, and innovation support.

◊

Researchers

Moves leverage points from diagnosing where interventions land to understanding what enables leaders to go deeper. Bridges futures consciousness with technology adoption literature.
