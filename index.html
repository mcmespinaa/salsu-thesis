Qualitative

RQ1 & RQ2

Case Selection

4-6 Nordic SMEs
Purposive sampling
B Corp, awards, networks

Replication Logic

2-3 deep LP cases
2-3 shallow LP cases
Cross-case comparison

(Yin, 2018)

Data Gathering

Document analysis
Semi-structured interviews

(Tsvetkova et al., 2020)

Thematic Analysis

6-phase approach (Braun & Clarke, 2006)
Deductive: 4 LP levels + 5 FC dims
Inductive: emergent themes

Cross-Case Analysis

How imagination-AI interactions differ between deep vs. shallow cases

RQ1: How do Nordic SME sustainability leaders use AI tools to envision and implement deeper sustainability interventions?
RQ2: What distinguishes imagination-AI interaction at deeper LP from SMEs at parameter level?

Shared Constructs Both perspectives use:
• 4 leverage levels (Riechers et al., 2022)
• 5 FC dimensions (Ahvenharju et al., 2018)
• AI use depth classification

For SME Leaders

Shift from "Does this improve performance?" to "Is this helping me optimise what exists or imagine what could be?" Concrete models of imagination-AI practice.

For Policymakers

Conditions for AI as transformative, not just optimising. Design implications for training, adoption guidance, innovation support.

For Researchers

Moves LP framework from diagnosis to enablers. Bridges FC with technology adoption. Tests cognitive capacity at organisational level mediated by AI.
